Word Analogy

AIM

A word can be categorized as either simple or complex. For instance, the word 'play' exemplifies simplicity as it exists as a standalone unit without any added components. Conversely, when we examine the word 'played', it becomes evident that it falls into the category of complexity. This complexity arises from the combination of the root 'play' with the past tense suffix '-ed'.

THEORY

An analysis of word complexity reveals that words can be categorized as either simple or complex. For instance, the word 'play' exemplifies simplicity as it exists as a standalone unit without any added components. Conversely, when we examine the word 'played', it becomes evident that it falls into the category of complexity. This complexity arises from the combination of the root 'play' with the past tense suffix '-ed'.

Morphological analysis involves dissecting a word into its root and affix(es), which is essential for various natural language processing tasks. Identifying the root of a word is critical across languages. For example, in English, the word 'play' can take different forms such as 'play', 'plays', 'played', and 'playing'.

Morphology can be categorized into two types:

  • Inflectional Morphology: This deals with word forms of a root without altering the lexical category. For instance, 'played' is an inflection of the verb 'play'.
  • Derivational Morphology: This deals with word forms of a root, resulting in a change in the lexical category. For example, 'happiness' is a derived noun form of the adjective 'happy'.

During morphological analysis, words are annotated with features such as lexical category, gender, number, person, tense, aspect, and case. These features aid in comprehending the structure and function of words within a language, thereby facilitating various linguistic analyses and natural language processing tasks.

Objective

The objective of the experiment is to learn about morphological features of a word by analyzing it.

Procedure

STEP 1: Select the word.

OUTPUT: Drop down for selecting features will appear.

STEP 2: Select the features.

STEP 3: Click "Check" button to check your answer.

OUTPUT: Right features are marked by tick and wrong features are marked by cross.
